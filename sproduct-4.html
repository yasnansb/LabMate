PCR buffer

This chemical solution maintains the optimal pH and salt conditions (often including magnesium ions) required for the Taq polymerase enzyme to function stably.

PCR buffer is a stabilized salt solution that provides the optimal chemical environment for Taq polymerase to function. Its core components are Tris-HCl (to maintain a stable pH of around 8.3–9.0), potassium chloride (KCl) (to facilitate primer annealing by neutralizing the negative charge on DNA), and magnesium chloride (MgCl₂) (an essential cofactor for Taq activity—Mg²⁺ concentration directly affects enzyme efficiency and primer specificity). Some formulations also include bovine serum albumin (BSA) or non-ionic detergents (like Tween 20) to prevent the enzyme from sticking to tube walls or to inhibit contaminants. Without this buffer, the pH would drift and the enzyme would have no catalytic activity, making amplification impossible.
