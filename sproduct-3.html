Deoxynucleotide Triphosphates (dNTPs)

The building blocks. These are the loose bases (A, T, C, and G) that Taq polymerase uses to construct the new DNA copies.

dNTPs (deoxynucleotide triphosphates) are the individual building blocks—dATP, dTTP, dCTP, and dGTP—that DNA polymerase uses to synthesize new DNA strands. Each one consists of a deoxyribose sugar, a phosphate group (triphosphate), and one of the four nitrogenous bases (A, T, C, or G). During PCR, Taq polymerase incorporates these free-floating dNTPs into the growing complementary strand, pairing them with the template bases (A with T, C with G). When the enzyme attaches a dNTP, it cleaves off two phosphate groups to release energy, leaving a single phosphate to form the backbone bond. For a standard PCR reaction, all four must be present in equal concentrations to ensure accurate and efficient amplification.
