Microtube Holder

Microcentrifuge tubes (also called Eppendorf tubes or "microfuge" tubes for short) are commonly used to hold and mix small amounts of liquid

A microtube holder is a rack or block designed to securely hold small microcentrifuge tubes (typically 0.5, 1.5, or 2.0 mL) upright on the bench during sample preparation, storage, or transfer. They come in various formats: rigid plastic or metal racks with numbered holes for organization, floating foam holders for water baths, chilled aluminum blocks for temperature-sensitive samples (kept on ice or cold packs), and magnetic racks for bead-based purification steps (e.g., DNA extraction). Some are also used as rotor inserts for centrifugation. Their main job is to keep tubes stable, accessible, and clearly arranged to prevent spills and mix-ups.
