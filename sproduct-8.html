PCR machine

Instrument used to amplify segments of DNA or RNA

A PCR machine (thermal cycler) is an instrument that automates the temperature cycling required for DNA amplification. It rapidly and precisely heats and cools a reaction block (holding tubes or 96/384-well plates) to execute the three main steps of PCR: denaturation (~95°C to separate DNA strands), annealing (~50–65°C for primer binding), and extension (~72°C for Taq polymerase to synthesize new DNA). It uses Peltier elements for accurate temperature control and a heated lid to prevent condensation and evaporation from the reaction tubes. Advanced models feature gradient functions to test multiple annealing temperatures simultaneously and fast ramping rates for quicker runs.
