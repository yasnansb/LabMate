Lab tube

An open-topped cylindrical container used in scientific and medical settings to hold, mix, or heat small quantities of substances

A lab tube is a general term for disposable plastic or glass containers used to hold, store, or process liquid samples. The most common are microcentrifuge tubes (0.5–2.0 mL, snap-cap, polypropylene) for DNA/RNA work and small-volume reactions; PCR tubes (0.2 mL, thin-walled for optimal heat transfer) used in thermal cyclers; and conical centrifuge tubes (15 mL or 50 mL, screw-cap) for larger volumes and centrifugation. They're typically made of autoclavable, chemically resistant polypropylene to withstand freezing, boiling, and high-speed spinning without cracking. Key features include graduation marks for volume estimation, frosted writing areas for labeling, and sterile, nuclease-free options for sensitive applications.
