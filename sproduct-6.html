Centrifuge

A mechanical device that uses rapid rotation to separate components of varying densities within a liquid or gas

User manual: Match Weights: Ensure every sample tube is paired with another tube of the exact same weight and liquid volume. If you have an odd number of samples, fill an empty tube with water to act as a dummy weight. Load Opposingly: Place the matching tubes directly across from each other in the rotor. Check Symmetry: Look down at the rotor to verify the layout is perfectly balanced and symmetrical around the center spindle before closing the lid. Purpose: A laboratory centrifuge uses centrifugal force by spinning liquid samples at high speeds to separate components of different densities. It rapidly forces denser materials to the bottom of a tube to isolate specific fractions—such as cells, organelles, proteins, or nucleic acids—from a liquid mixture.
