Taq polymerase

This is a heat-stable enzyme that builds the new DNA strands by physically linking loose genetic building blocks together.

Taq polymerase is a heat-stable DNA polymerase enzyme derived from the bacterium Thermus aquaticus, which lives in hot springs. Its key feature is its ability to withstand the high temperatures (around 95°C) used during the denaturation step of PCR without denaturing itself. It functions by synthesizing new DNA strands, extending primers that anneal to a template strand by adding nucleotides in the 5' to 3' direction. However, it lacks 3' to 5' exonuclease proofreading activity, meaning it has a relatively high error rate, but its thermostability and efficiency make it the standard enzyme for routine PCR amplification.
